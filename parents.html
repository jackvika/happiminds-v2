Parenting Tips from Our Experts

Practical advice for everyday brain-building moments

Transform everyday spaces into learning opportunities. Keep stimulating materials within reach, create quiet corners for focus, and establish predictable routines that help children feel secure while providing novelty to spark curiosity.

  • Rotate toys and books weekly to maintain interest
  • Use visual schedules to build executive function
  • Incorporate sensory elements like textured rugs or calming lights

Play is the work of childhood. Through play, children develop cognitive flexibility, problem-solving skills, and emotional resilience. Join your child in play to deepen connections while guiding their development.

  • Follow your child's lead in play to understand their interests
  • Ask open-ended questions to extend their thinking
  • Introduce new vocabulary during play scenarios
  • Use household items creatively to spark imagination

Help your child navigate big emotions by becoming an emotional coach. This approach builds emotional intelligence while strengthening your bond.

  • Name emotions as they arise ("I see you're feeling frustrated")
  • Validate feelings without immediately trying to fix them
  • Teach calming techniques like belly breathing
  • Problem-solve together once emotions have settled
  • Model healthy emotional expression yourself

Executive functions like working memory, flexible thinking, and self-control are better predictors of school success than IQ. Strengthen these skills through daily interactions.

  • Break tasks into smaller steps with clear instructions
  • Play memory games like "I went to the market and bought..."
  • Encourage problem-solving by asking "What could we try?"
  • Use timers and visual cues to build time management
  • Practice waiting and turn-taking in low-stakes situations

Parenting Questions Answered

How often should I practice these activities with my child?

We recommend short, daily sessions of 10-15 minutes for best results. Consistency is more important than duration.

What if I can't attend the live workshops?

All registered participants receive workshop recordings and materials. You can also download our free guides or schedule a private consultation.

How soon will I see results from these techniques?

Most parents notice improvements in focus and emotional regulation within 4-6 weeks of consistent practice.
